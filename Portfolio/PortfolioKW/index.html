About me

Hello, my name is Katharine Wilson. Welcome to my portfolio! I am an aspiring politics and general news reporter from Riverside, CA studying journalism and government & politics at The University of Maryland.

I have experience covering multiple levels of politics (local, county, state, and federal) for digital and print publications. I will cover the federal government in Capital News Service’s Washington D.C. Bureau in the spring of 2024.

As an intern for Montgomery Community Media, I reported on county politics, education, and the environment. Last summer, I wrote for The Herald-Mail in Hagerstown, MD, covering how national politics impacts everyday people - including stories on cannabis legalization, immigration, and aging.

My other internships have included reporting on a local city council for College Park Here & Now and researching for the newsletter-based service News Not Noise. I wrote a widely published enterprise story on pandemic learning loss and collaborated on a statewide school voter guide as a reporter for the Local News Network.

I have been a staff writer for my university’s independent student publication, The Diamondback, since my sophomore year. My reporting has focused on city development, the College Park City Council, protests, local elections, and local businesses.

When not reporting on politics, my other main interest in journalism is art and entertainment reporting. I am the producer of WMUC-FM Radio’s entertainment news show “Drop the Pop,” where I manage a team of reporters and commentators. I have also reported on local artists for The Herald-Mail, Montgomery Community Media, and The Diamondback.

After graduation, I plan to spend my journalism career humanizing national politics through feature stories, holding governmental officials accountable through investigations, and helping people understand and improve their community.
